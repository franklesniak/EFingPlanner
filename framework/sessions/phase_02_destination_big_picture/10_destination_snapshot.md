# Session 10: Destination Snapshot

You are here: Phase 2 (Destination Big Picture), First Taste step 5 of 13.
Previous: [05 Good Sources, Bad Sources](../phase_01_research_skills/05_good_sources_bad_sources.md) | Next: [12 Weather, Seasons, and Events](12_weather_seasons_and_events.md)

**For parents:**

- Status: Core
- Planner skill: organizing information
- Estimated time: 20-30 minutes
- Parent involvement: 5-minute check-in
- Materials: a device with the kid-safe filter on, your Source Log, your Trip-Basics card

## Goal

Make one snapshot page of the big facts about Japan.

## Start Here

Write "Japan" at the top of a fresh page and draw a box for your snapshot. That is your
start.

## Steps

Before you plan *where* to go, it helps to know a few big facts. You do not need to
memorize anything -- this is just a snapshot to get oriented.

Fill in your snapshot page. Look up anything you are not sure about, and add it to your
Source Log.

1. **Capital:** Japan's capital is Tokyo.
2. **Major islands:** the four big ones are Honshu, Hokkaido, Kyushu, and Shikoku.
3. **Currency:** the yen.
4. **Main language:** Japanese.
5. **Time difference:** use the hours-ahead figure on your Trip-Basics card. For families
   in the US, Japan is roughly 14 to 15 hours ahead -- about half a day. A grown-up can
   confirm your exact number.
6. **Getting there:** it is a long flight from the US. A grown-up can tell you roughly how
   long from your home airport. (Flights are the grown-ups' job.)
7. **Three surprising facts:** find three things about Japan that surprise you, from a
   trusted source. Write them down.
8. **One question for later:** write one thing you want to find out. Park it for now.

## Workspace

Use your snapshot page for the facts, and your Source Log for wherever the three
surprising facts came from.

You can say your answers to an adult who writes them, or draw them, if that is easier.

## Artifact Created

Your Destination snapshot page: the big facts about Japan, plus three surprising facts
and one question for later.

## Stop Point

You are done when your snapshot has the capital, islands, currency, language, time
difference, three surprising facts, and one question. Do not try to learn everything about
Japan today -- a snapshot is enough.

## Source Check

Record the source of your three surprising facts in your Source Log: title, who made it,
the web address or book page, and today's date.

## Finish and Quality Check

Finished? Use the [Finish and Quality Check card](../../student_guide/finish_and_quality_check.md) in your student guide.

## If You Get Stuck

Stuck? Use the [When I'm Stuck card](../../student_guide/when_im_stuck.md) in your student guide.

## Optional Extension

If you have extra energy, find today's *exact* time difference between your home and Japan
yourself, and write down why it can change (hint: daylight saving time). It is a small
"facts have conditions" check. If not, you are done.

## Parent Notes

The goal is orientation, not mastery -- a snapshot, not a report. The time difference
comes from your Trip-Basics card so the session stays reusable; do not expect a pinned
number here. If your child gets pulled into a fun rabbit hole, that is fine for a few
minutes, then steer back to finishing the snapshot. Keep flight specifics as "a grown-up
handles that."
