# Session 14: Checkpoint 1 Season Recommendation

You are here: Phase 2 (Destination Big Picture), First Taste step 8 of 13. **This is Checkpoint 1 -- your first family decision.** Previous: [13 Trip Goals and Travel Style](13_trip_goals_and_travel_style.md) | Next: [15 City Research Cards](../phase_03_choose_places/15_city_research_cards.md)

**For parents:**

- Status: Core -- **Checkpoint 1** (a real review; parent-gated)
- Planner skill: making trade-offs; making a recommendation
- Estimated time: 20-30 minutes for the child, plus a 20-40 minute review with you
- Parent involvement: parent review -- genuinely use the recommendation in a real family talk
- Materials: your Season comparison chart, your Source Log, a [Decision Record page](../../templates/decision_record.md)

## Goal

Recommend the best time for your family to visit Japan, and write it down as your first real decision.

## Start Here

Write "My season recommendation" at the top of a fresh page. You are starting your first recommendation.

## Steps

You have compared the seasons. Now you make a recommendation -- a real one the grown-ups will use. A good recommendation gives a clear pick *and* the reasons behind it.

On a [Decision Record page](../../templates/decision_record.md), write:

1. **Best season** for the trip, in your opinion.
2. **Backup season** (your second choice).
3. **A season or time to be careful about**, and why (for example, a rainy or very crowded window).
4. **Possible months**, if your research points to any. "Not sure yet" is okay.
5. **Your reasons** -- two or three sentences. This is the important part.
6. **Your sources** -- from your Source Log.
7. **Questions for the grown-ups** (for example, "Does this fit school and work?").

Then bring it to a grown-up for a real family talk. They will weigh school and work schedules, weather, crowds, cost, and holidays. They may agree, or adjust it with you -- either way, your work shaped the decision.

**This is real progress.** Once your season is set, your family knows *when* you might go. The trip is becoming real, and you helped decide it.

*A calm choice point:* this is a natural moment to decide to keep going, take a break, or stop -- and all three are okay. There is no wrong choice here.

*One thing to know (mostly for the grown-ups):* a few must-do experiences and popular hotels can fill up months ahead. You do not have to lock in dates now -- just know that waiting a long time can make some options harder to get.

## Workspace

Use your Decision Record page. This becomes the first entry in your **decision log** -- a place you will record each big decision and its reasons all through the project.

You can say your answers to an adult who writes them, or draw them, if that is easier.

## Artifact Created

Your Season recommendation report (your first decision-log entry): a season pick, a backup, a caution, reasons, sources, and questions for the grown-ups.

## Stop Point

You are done when you have a season pick with reasons and sources, and you have shared it with a grown-up. You do not need exact dates -- a season and maybe some months is a complete recommendation.

## Source Check

List the sources behind your recommendation, from your Source Log. If any fact (like a festival date) could change, note that a grown-up should re-check it close to travel.

## Finish and Quality Check

Finished? Use the [Finish and Quality Check card](../../student_guide/finish_and_quality_check.md) in your student guide.

## If You Get Stuck

Stuck? Use the [When I'm Stuck card](../../student_guide/when_im_stuck.md) in your student guide.

## Optional Extension

If you have extra energy, write one line about *why* you did not pick your backup season. Explaining a choice you did *not* make is a real planner skill. If not, you are done.

## Parent Notes

This is the pilot's big milestone -- reaching a real season recommendation, largely on your child's own. Genuinely take their recommendation into a real family conversation; seeing their work shape the trip this early is what sustains months of effort. Deliver any change warmly ("we're going to shift this because of school -- your reasons still hold"). This is also the try-then-commit decision point: keep going, pause, or park it -- all are real successes. If your child is still lukewarm here, see the [coaching guide](../../parent_guide/coaching_and_support.md) rather than pushing on. Record the final family decision on the same page so your decision log and the checkpoints stay in sync.
