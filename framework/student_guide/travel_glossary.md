# Travel Glossary

New words, explained simply. If you meet a travel word you do not know, look here first, or
ask a grown-up to help you look it up. Each session also explains new words the first time
they show up.

## Travel words (any trip)

- **Itinerary** -- your day-by-day plan for the trip.
- **Route** -- the order of the places you visit.
- **Shortlist** -- a short list of your best choices, picked from a longer list.
- **Trade-off** -- when choosing one thing means giving up another.
- **Source** -- where a fact came from (a website, book, map, or person).
- **Peak season** -- the busiest, usually priciest time to visit.
- **Day trip** -- visiting a place for the day and coming back to sleep in your base city.

## Japan words you will meet

- **Shinkansen** -- Japan's fast "bullet train" between big cities.
- **IC card** -- a tap-to-pay card for trains and buses (names and options change; a
  grown-up checks what is available now).
- **Konbini** -- a convenience store (like 7-Eleven). Bright, staffed, open long hours,
  and very handy.
- **Koban** -- a small neighborhood police box where you can ask for help.
- **Ryokan** -- a traditional inn with tatami-mat rooms and futon bedding.
- **Onsen** -- a hot-spring bath. There are polite rules; a grown-up helps you learn them.
- **Izakaya** -- a casual Japanese pub-restaurant (a grown-up decides if it fits your family).
- **Takkyubin** -- luggage forwarding: your bags are sent ahead so you do not carry them on
  stairs.
- **Gachapon** -- capsule-toy machines you will see all over.
- **Goshuin** -- a special stamp you can collect at shrines and temples (visit respectfully
  first).

## Numbers you will see in Japan

- **Temperature is in Celsius (degC).** Rough guide: 30 is hot, 20 is mild, 10 is cool, 0
  is freezing. To get Fahrenheit, "double it and add 30" (close enough).
- **Distance is in kilometers (km).** A kilometer is a little more than half a mile.
- **Time uses a 24-hour clock.** 14:00 means 2 p.m. After noon, subtract 12.
- **Money is in yen.** To judge if something is expensive, convert to dollars -- but rates
  change, so look up today's rate and write the date. (Example only, check today's rate:
  about 100 yen = a bit under 1 US dollar.)
