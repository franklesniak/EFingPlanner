# Planner Mindset

How a good planner thinks. Read this once near the start, and come back to it when a
session feels hard.

## Being off is normal

Your first guesses about time, cost, and energy will probably be off -- everyone's are,
even grown-ups'. Being off is normal and expected. The point is to practice estimating and
then compare, not to be exactly right. Every time you guess and then check, your guessing
gets a little better.

## Good enough is good enough

Your job is not to research forever. Your job is to collect enough good information to make
a thoughtful recommendation. When you hit a stop point, you are done -- extra questions go
in your [question parking lot](../templates/question_parking_lot.md), not into endless
searching.

## Frustration is information

Feeling frustrated in the middle of a session is normal. Noticing it -- and taking a break
instead of quitting -- is a real planner skill. Stand up, get water, come back. The work
will still be there.

## You are a real planner

The trip is real, and your recommendations matter. It is also low-stakes: grown-ups make
every big decision, and "let's park this for later" is always okay. Your job is to help the
grown-ups decide better -- not to get everything perfect.
