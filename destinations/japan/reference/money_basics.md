# Money Basics (Japan)

**Last reviewed:** July 2026

Enough to help the child think about cost. Adults own the real budget, the actual exchange,
and all payment.

## The currency is the yen

To judge whether something is expensive, convert yen to dollars. But **rates move a lot from
year to year**, and any conversion is only true for the day you checked it -- so look up
today's rate and write the date beside it.

*Example only -- check today's rate:* recently it took very roughly 150-160 yen to make 1 US
dollar, so 100 yen was worth a bit more than half a US dollar. Rates move a lot -- do not rely
on this number; look up today's rate and write the date beside it.

## Cash still matters

Card and phone payments are growing, but **many small places are still cash-only** --
small restaurants, temple and shrine admissions, some local shops. So families plan to carry
some yen. Convenience-store ATMs (for example at 7-Eleven and Japan Post) can get yen with a
foreign card. The actual getting and carrying of cash is a grown-up's job. Treat "how cashed
or cashless a place is" as something to check for current norms.

## Costs come in different shapes

- **Per person** -- like meals and most activity tickets.
- **Per room** -- like most hotels. A family may need more than one room (Japanese rooms
  often cap how many people fit), which is why room count is an adult decision.
- **Per group** -- some things are one cost for everyone.
- A **ryokan** (traditional inn) is usually priced **per person and often includes dinner and
  breakfast**, so the per-room math does not apply to it.

## A fun budget fact to check

On Japanese trains, children often cost less than adults -- sometimes about half, and very
young children often ride free. Look up the current ages and amounts on an official source
before relying on it; do not assume a fixed rule.

## Things that are changing (verify, never assume)

Several travel-related costs are in motion: a departure tax, tax-free-shopping mechanics for
visitors, some city lodging or bathing taxes, and tourist/dual pricing at some attractions.
These change, so a grown-up verifies the current amounts and rules at booking and before
travel. No amount is pinned here on purpose.
