# Seasons, Weather, and Events (Japan)

**Last reviewed:** July 2026

Use this to compare seasons, not to pick "the perfect" one. These are stable patterns, but exact dates, prices, and forecasts move -- so **confirm this year's dates** for anything you plan around.

## The four seasons at a glance

- **Spring** -- mild, with cherry blossoms; very popular.
- **Summer** -- hot and humid, with a rainy stretch; long walking days are tiring.
- **Fall** -- cool and comfortable, with colorful leaves; popular.
- **Winter** -- cold, with snow in the north; quieter in many places.

## Things to watch for

- **Cherry blossoms** (spring) and **fall colors** (autumn) are big draws and busier times.
- **Rainy season** is roughly June for most of Japan.
- **Summer heat and humidity** are real and can be a health concern on long walking days -- for the child and for anyone who tires more easily. Plan summer days gently, with water.
- **Typhoon season** runs roughly late summer into autumn. A grown-up watches the official forecast; a forecast typhoon can shut trains and attractions for a day or two, so it is a reshuffle-the-days event, not just a rainy afternoon.

## Busy travel windows (crowds and higher prices -- confirm this year's dates)

- **Golden Week** -- roughly late April into early May.
- **Obon** -- roughly mid-August.
- **New Year** -- roughly late December into early January, when many places also close.

## The cherry-blossom timing trap

You can plan for the *season*, but no one can promise the exact peak week months ahead. Peak shifts a week or more year to year, and forecasts only firm up in late winter. That is normal and fine: grown-ups book on the usual averages, keep day-by-day plans flexible, and book refundable where possible. You do not have to control the exact day.

## What stays with the adults

Weather alerts, typhoon monitoring, and travel advisories are adult-owned. The child's job is to compare seasons and recommend a good time; adults confirm dates and watch the weather.
